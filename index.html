Science

I work on various topics of theoretical astrophysics research, including

  • Planetary astrophysics: Planet formation and migration in protoplanetary disks; Observable signatures of planet-disk interactions; Dust evolution in protoplanetary disks; Planet atmospheres and spectra
  • General Processes in Accretion Disks: Gravitational Instability and MRI in accretion disks; Formation and evolution of Stars and Black Holes embedded in AGN Disks; Tidally distorted and warped accretion disks

Miscellaneous

I enjoy science fiction and fantasy, which is part of the reason I chose to pursue a career in Astrophysics in the first place. As a hardcore Whovian, I translated Feast of the Drowned, an official Doctor Who spin-off novel into Chinese on an online forum when I was 12. A Wicked Letter Through Time (邪恶的时光信笺) is a short sci-fi film I written, produced and co-directed for THU Physics Student Gala 2019. It was well-received by audiences in department.

I am an enthusiast of applying modern phonetics (e.g. the IPA framework) to study how ancient Chinese pronunciation has evolved over the years. This helped me systematically understand many basic logics behind pronunciations of Chinese dialects such as Cantonese, and also the onyomi components of Japanese. Happy to talk about East Asian Languages!
